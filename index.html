Google Scholar Profile

I am an assistant professor in the R. B. Annis School of Engineering at the University of Indianapolis. I have years of experience in the industry and academy, which involved recommendation systems, natural language processing, the Internet of Things, big data, system design and embedded systems. My research covers , artificial intelligence, computer science education, and intelligent systems. With my research interests and industrial background, my efforts have mainly been put into applying artificial intelligence in software engineering; in a way, new approaches or tools can be created and engineers' behaviors can be further understood. In my past research, source codes were mined across multiple repositories to increase the understanding of software reuses among projects. Patterns were identified from the project implementation activities to discover the differences and similarities between agile and plan-driven software developments. To handle complexity, I created a synthesis-based approach for software system designs.

Studying software development can aid computer science education and intelligence systems in many aspects. Improving the knowledge of engineering principles or developing intelligent tools to support students' learning relies on research in software development. Intelligent systems are complex due to communication among inner parts and external systems. Investigating the design activities or methods is helpful to engineers who would like to develop an intelligent system efficiently.
